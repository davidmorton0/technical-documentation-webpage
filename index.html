Ruby Operators

Ruby has different operators which are used to do things to values.

  • Arithmetic Operators
  • Comparison Operators
  • Assignment Operators
  • Bitwise Operators
  • Logical Operators
Arithmetic Operators

The Arithmetic Operators are +, -, *, /, % and **

Example: 4 + 5 gives 9

The Arithmetic Operators work the same ways as in maths.

Comparison Operators

The Comparison Operators are ==, !=, >, <, >=, <=, <=>, ===, eql? and equal?

Example: 4 > 5 gives false

Comparison Operators are for comparing numbers and other objects.

Assignment Operators

The Assignment Operators are = +=, -=, *=, /=, %= and **=

Example: a += 5 will add 5 to the value stored in a.

Assignment Operators are for storing values in variables, often with an operation using the already stored value first.


Bitwise Operators

The Bitwise Operators are &, |, *, ~ << and >>

Example: 5 | 11 gives 15 which is the same as 0101 | 1011 giving 1111.

The Bitwise operators are used for bit by bit operations.


Logical Operators

The Logical Operators are 'and', 'or', &&, ||, ! and 'not'.

Example: true || false gives true.

The Logical Operators are used for combining true/false expressions.
